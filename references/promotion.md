# Promotion, Launch & Marketing Reference

## Series Strategy — The Long Game

The most profitable model in self-publishing is a series with strong read-through.

### Read-Through Economics
```
Book 1 price: $0.99 (or permafree)
Books 2–5 price: $4.99 each

If 30% of Book 1 readers buy Book 2:
  Each Book 1 reader = $0.99 + (0.30 × $4.99) = $2.49 average value

If 25% read-through to Book 5:
  Each Book 1 reader = $0.99 + (0.30 × $4.99) + (0.25 × $4.99) + ... ≈ $4–6 LTV
```

**Rule**: price Book 1 as a loss leader. The back catalogue is where you earn.

### Series Pricing Strategy
- Book 1: Permafree or $0.99 (gets people into the series)
- Book 2: $2.99 (lower barrier for first paid purchase)
- Books 3+: $3.99–$4.99 (full price — readers are committed)
- Box set (Books 1–3): $7.99–$9.99 (adds value, separate product)

## Permafree Strategy

A permanently free book is the most powerful reader acquisition tool in indie publishing.
Amazon does not allow $0.00 directly — use price matching.

### How to Make a Book Permafree on Amazon

1. Set price to $0.00 on all other platforms (Apple Books, Kobo, B&N, Google Play via D2D or PublishDrive)
2. Wait for Amazon to detect the lower price (can take 2–8 weeks)
3. Report the lower price to Amazon via KDP support ("price match request")
4. Amazon matches to $0.00 — "permafree" is now live

**Note**: Cannot do permafree while enrolled in KDP Select (which requires exclusivity).

### Permafree on Google Play
- Free downloads on Google Play count towards chart rankings
- Strong for series Book 1 — ranking boosts discovery
- Set to $0.00 directly in your Google Play publisher account or via PublishDrive

### Maintaining Permafree
- Monitor regularly — Amazon sometimes re-prices to $0.99 if others raise price
- Keep all other platforms at $0.00 to maintain the price match

## BookBub

BookBub is the most powerful book promotion platform. A Featured Deal is transformative.

### Featured Deal Requirements
- Book must be **wide** (not KDP Select exclusive) — BookBub now rarely accepts exclusive books
- Strong reviews: minimum ~10, ideally 50+, 4.0+ average
- Cover must look professional and genre-appropriate
- Price must be discounted: Free, $0.99, or $1.99 for most genres
- Application via bookbub.com/partners — acceptance rate is low (~20%)

### BookBub Featured Deal Impact
- Typical results: 10,000–50,000+ downloads in 24 hours (depending on genre)
- Drives Amazon rank dramatically → organic visibility for 1–2 weeks after
- Series read-through: massive if Book 1 is the deal title

### If You Don't Get a Featured Deal: Alternatives
- **Freebooksy** (free ebooks): freebooksy.com — lower cost, good volume
- **Bargain Booksy** ($0.99 ebooks): bargainbooksy.com
- **Robin Reads**: robinreads.com
- **Ereader News Today (ENT)**: ereadernewstoday.com
- **BookSends**: booksends.com
- **The Fussy Librarian**: thefussylibrarian.com
- **ManyBooks**: manybooks.net

Stack multiple sites on the same day for maximum effect.

## ARC (Advance Review Copy) Strategy

Reviews at launch are critical — aim for 10–20 before launch day.

### Building an ARC List
1. Add an ARC opt-in to your existing ebook back matter: "Want to read my next book free? Join my ARC team at [URL]"
2. Post in Facebook groups for your genre (search "[genre] ARC readers" or "[genre] book club")
3. Use NetGalley ($450/title) for traditionally-distributed ARCs to librarians and reviewers
4. Use BookSirens or Booksprout (cheaper ARC platforms with reader communities)

### ARC Timing
- Send ARCs 4–6 weeks before launch
- Set review deadline: 1 week before launch day
- Remind at 2 weeks and 3 days before deadline
- Ask ARCs to post on launch day (helps Amazon algorithm) — not before (Amazon may remove early reviews)

### What NOT to Do
- Never ask for positive reviews — ask for honest reviews
- Never offer payment for reviews — Amazon TOS violation
- Never include review requests inside the ebook itself — Amazon TOS violation
- Do not email people who haven't opted in to your ARC list

## Pre-Launch (4–6 Weeks Before)

- [ ] Email list: early access offer or "tell me you want to be notified"
- [ ] ARC readers: 10–20 confirmed, ARCs sent
- [ ] Amazon Author Central: bio, photo, claimed titles set up
- [ ] Cover reveal: social post + email teaser
- [ ] Pre-order set up on KDP (if going wide: D2D pre-order populates all platforms)
- [ ] Press kit: cover image (PNG), bio, key quotes, description, author photo
- [ ] Social content calendar: 3–4 posts per week in run-up

---

## Launch Week

- [ ] **Email sequence** (3 emails over 5 days):
  - Day 1: "It's live — here's the link"
  - Day 3: "What readers are saying" (share first ARC quotes)
  - Day 5: "Last chance at launch price" (if running a time-limited price)
- [ ] **Social posts**: cover, behind-the-scenes, reader reactions
- [ ] **KDP promotion** (if KDP Select): Countdown Deal or Free Days — maximise downloads day 1
- [ ] **Promo sites**: submit to Freebooksy, Bargain Booksy, ENT (if discounting)
- [ ] **BookBub**: if approved, coordinate with launch (or run 2–4 weeks after for a "second wave")
- [ ] **Outreach**: 3–5 podcast/newsletter partners in your genre — offer an excerpt or interview
- [ ] **Amazon Author Central**: ensure book is claimed, add editorial review if you have any

---

## Post-Launch

- [ ] **Reviews**: email list at Day 14 — "If you've finished the book, an honest review means the world"
- [ ] **A/B test**: try 2 different cover thumbnails using Amazon's A/B testing tool (if available)
- [ ] **Description optimisation**: test different hooks, update based on what readers say they loved
- [ ] **Also Bought**: check who appears in "Customers Also Bought" — reach out to those authors for cross-promotion
- [ ] **Annual refresh**: update statistics, examples, links — re-promote as "Updated Edition"
- [ ] **Expand formats**: if ebook only → add print → add audio → add translation

## Kindle Unlimited (KDP Select) Mechanics

If enrolled in KDP Select, page reads earn royalties from the KU Global Fund.

- **KENPC** (Kindle Edition Normalized Page Count): Amazon's measure of page count, calculated from font size + settings — not actual pages. A 300-page book ≈ 400–500 KENPC.
- **Rate**: approximately $0.004–$0.005 per KENPC page read (varies monthly — declared by Amazon)
- **Break-even vs sale**: roughly 200–250 KENPC reads = equivalent of one $0.99 sale at 35% royalty
- **Strategy**: KU is best for fiction readers who read 5+ books/month and are loyal to KU

**KU vs Wide — rule of thumb**:
- Fiction (especially romance, fantasy, thriller): often better in KU due to binge-reading patterns
- Non-fiction, business, self-help: usually better wide (readers buy, not subscribe)

## Email List Building Through Books

The back matter of every ebook is prime real estate for list building.

### Back Matter CTA Sequence
```
1. "Thank you for reading" page
2. Request for review (brief, 1 paragraph)
3. "Also by the author" list (with links)
4. Lead magnet offer: "Get [complementary resource] free at [URL]"
5. Newsletter sign-up: "Join 10,000+ readers who get [benefit] weekly"
6. Author bio + social links
```

### Lead Magnet Ideas for Books
- Extended chapter or bonus content not in the book
- Companion workbook or templates
- Resource list / tool kit related to the book's topic
- First chapter of the next book in the series
- Exclusive short story (fiction)
